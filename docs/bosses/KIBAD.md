# KIBAD — Rogue Angel

**Zone:** Shadow Core  
**Type:** Rogue Angel  
**Hunter Echo:** Dabik — his direct dimensional inversion. Same soul. Different world. Different choice. Where Dabik became shadow, Kibad became light. Both are the most dangerous thing in any room they enter. One because you cannot find him. The other because you cannot look away.

---

## Quote

> *"I was made to carry the light. Nobody warned me what it costs to burn that long."*
> — Kibad. No archive. No record. The words exist only because someone was close enough to hear them before the fight began.

---

## Lore

The gates did not only open doors between places.

They opened doors between versions.

In one world, a tracker's grandson from a small island between the Caribbean Sea and the Atlantic became Dabik. Shadow. Precision. The most dangerous thing is what you never see coming. He chose the dark not because it was easy but because the dark protects — you cannot be touched by what cannot find you.

In another world, the same blood. The same instincts. The same fork in the road.

But the light won.

He was celestial from the beginning — not chosen, not transformed, simply *what he was* when the gate energy of his world recognised what lived in him and answered it. He became the light-bringer. Radiant. Overwhelming. The kind of presence that fills a room before he enters it and stays after he leaves.

In his world, that made him essential. Then it made him useful. Then it made him a weapon.

Being the light in a dark world does not make you a hero for long. It makes you a target first, then a tool, then something that gets directed and deployed and spent. He served because that was what light was supposed to do. He held back because that was what angels were supposed to do.

Until he stopped.

What he did when he stopped holding back is not recorded in any text that survived. What is known is the consequence — he was banished. Cast from his world into the dark. Not destroyed. Not killed. Exiled into the worst possible place for something made of light:

Pure void.

The Shadow Core.

He arrived through the dimensional rift the gates tore open between worlds and landed in the darkest place in this one. The gravitational pull between opposites — the void recognising the light and drawing it in, the way a wound draws pressure. He did not choose this zone. It claimed him.

He hates it here.

He has always hated it here. Every moment in the Shadow Core is an angel surrounded by the one thing he was never built to endure. He does not corrupt the darkness. He does not rule it. He rages against it — blinds everything around him because the alternative is letting the dark win, and he has never once in any world been willing to let the dark win.

His name is Kibad.

Dabik, read backwards. Whether that is coincidence or the universe's cruelest editorial comment is a question that has no answer.

What matters is this: when Dabik enters the Shadow Core, something shifts. Two versions of the same soul in the same space for the first time. Kibad does not know what Dabik is. But he feels it — the shadow that mirrors his light, the human that carries the inverse of everything he is.

He fights because he has been fighting since he arrived. He fights because the dark is still there and he is still made of light and he does not know how to stop.

**But when Dabik defeats him — when the shadow finally takes the light in — Kibad understands what is happening.**

Not defeat. Completion.

For the first time since he was cast down, the dark does not feel like exile. The shadow that absorbs him is warm. Familiar. The other half of what he was always supposed to be.

He smiles.

And he ascends.

The Shadow Core goes completely dark for two seconds — every light source, every particle, every aura. Then Kibad rises upward through the void, slow, silent, trailing white-gold light that fades as he goes. Not dying. Leaving. Going somewhere the banishment no longer applies.

Dabik stands in the dark and does not fully understand what just happened.

But his aura is different now. Still black, still deep purple at the edges. But there is a trace of gold in it that was not there before. It never leaves. The UI does not explain it. There is no tooltip. No achievement.

It is just there.

## Appearance

Kibad is Dabik printed in light. The silhouette is immediate — same lean build, same twin daggers, same controlled stillness. Then the palette hits and everything is wrong in the most beautiful possible way.

- **Body:** Lean, controlled, precise. Moves with the same economy of motion as Dabik — nothing wasted, nothing decorative. Same build, same height, same stance.
- **Skin:** Pale and luminous — not sickly, almost radiant. Like something lit from inside.
- **Hair:** Short, wild, spiky — same shape as Dabik's white hair, but pure black. The one detail that reads immediately as inversion rather than copy.
- **Eyes:** Gold-white, flat, no pupils visible at full power. Bright but not warm. Unsettling because they do not read as human. They are the eyes of something that has seen too much light for too long.
- **Clothing:** White and pale gold fitted layers — same cut as Dabik's dark tactical layers, exact mirror palette. Clean. Too clean. The clothing of someone who was never supposed to be here.
- **Daggers:** Same curved shape as Dabik's. Polished bone-white with gold edge lines. Pristine. They have never looked used even though they have been.
- **Aura:** White-gold that radiates outward — not controlled like Dabik's shadow aura that spreads quiet and close. Kibad's bleeds wide, too much, overwhelming. In the Shadow Core it creates a stark contrast — a blinding white-gold presence in absolute void. Feels like standing too close to something celestial. Feels like a warning.

## Combat

Kibad fights the way Dabik fights — with purpose and precision. But where Dabik ends encounters quietly, Kibad ends them with overwhelming force. He is not trying to be efficient. He is trying to stop the dark from closing in.

### Phase 1 — 100–60% HP
Measured. Precise. Every movement deliberate.

| Attack | Description | CD | Telegraph | Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Blink Slash | Teleports behind nearest player, single precise strike | 5s | Half-second stillness, aura pulls inward | Predict blink direction |
| Dagger Combo | Four-hit sequence, fast, each hit slightly brighter than the last | 4s | Shifts weight forward, gold flickers on blades | Back off / dodge through |

### Phase 2 — 60–0% HP
The banishment takes hold. Kibad stops holding back.

| Attack | Description | CD | Telegraph | Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Twin Clones | Two light-form decoys appear — same movement, one real | 10s | Aura splits outward in two directions | Watch eye direction — clones have no eyes |
| Radiant Burst | Full arena flash, damages all players caught in light | 8s | Kibad goes completely still, aura maximises, one beat of silence | Full dodge / behind cover |
| Blink Slash+ | Chains two consecutive blinks before striking | 5s | Same telegraph, faster execution | Predict second blink |

**Phase transition:** The Shadow Core dims further. Kibad rises slightly off the ground — not floating dramatically, just no longer quite standing on it. The aura expands to fill the zone edges. The gold in his eyes brightens until the white disappears.

**Counter hint:** Benzu shoulder charge breaks his blink rhythm. Surge ultimate sync in Phase 2.

### Co-op Scaling
- 2P: +50% HP
- 3P: +100% HP + clones gain basic attack
- 4P: +150% HP + Radiant Burst pulses twice

## Death — The Ascension

When the final hit lands, Kibad does not fall.

He stops. The daggers lower. The aura pulls inward slowly — not collapsing, settling. The gold-white eyes focus on Dabik for one beat. Not hostile. Not afraid.

He smiles.

The Shadow Core goes completely dark. Every light source out. Every particle gone. Every aura suppressed — including the players'. Two full seconds of absolute void.

Then Kibad rises.

Slow. Silent. Upward through the dark, trailing white-gold light that fades as he ascends, like the last of a fire going out warm instead of cold. Not dying. Leaving. The banishment no longer holds because the shadow that was always supposed to meet him finally did.

The light fades. The Shadow Core's ambient lighting returns.

Dabik's aura has a trace of gold in it that was not there before.

The exit portal opens.

---

## Design Notes

- The clone tells in Phase 2 are intentional: clones mirror every movement perfectly but have no eyes. Players who notice will survive. Players who don't will guess.
- The two seconds of total darkness before the ascension should be absolute — no UI, no health bars, no particles. The only total blackout in the entire game.
- Dabik's gold aura trace is permanent for the rest of the session. It is a cosmetic change only. It is never explained.
- If Dabik is not in the session, the ascension still happens. Kibad still smiles. The gold trace appears on whoever dealt the killing blow — faint, brief, gone after the zone ends. Only Dabik keeps it.
- The music in the ascension sequence should be the only moment in the game with no percussion. Just tone. Just space.
