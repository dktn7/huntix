# VRAEL — Fire Bruiser

**Zone:** City Breach  
**Type:** Fire Bruiser  
**Hunter Echo:** Vesol — fire without precision. Vesol is a researcher who became flame through discipline. Vrael is a lab assistant who chased that same transformation through desperation and ego. Same world. Same gates. Opposite outcome.

---

## Quote

> *"They said I had potential. For once in my life, someone said that."*
> — Vrael, early mission debrief recording. File since classified.

---

## Lore

Before the gates opened, nobody noticed him. That was the whole problem.

His name was Cael Vrenne. Mid-level lab assistant at a privately contracted gate research facility — one of dozens that appeared in the first wave of funding after gate energy was confirmed as harvestable. He catalogued samples, ran calibration checks, filed reports nobody read. Quiet. Competent. Completely invisible.

When the facility began trials on controlled gate energy absorption in human subjects, Cael was the one who prepped the equipment. He watched A-Rank hunters walk in, absorb measured doses, walk out stronger. He understood the process better than half the researchers above him. He had read every file. He knew the risks.

He volunteered anyway.

The first absorption worked. Then the second. By the third mission the facility was publishing results — a new class of enhanced operative, gate energy integrated directly into the body, controllable output, measurable yield. Cael became known. Not famous, but known. His face appeared in internal briefings. Senior researchers remembered his name. He went on gate-clearing missions as a sanctioned test operative and came back each time with higher readings and better results.

He should have stopped there.

But for someone who had been invisible his entire life, being seen felt like the only thing that mattered. He pushed the absorption further. Requested unsanctioned top-ups between missions. Began entering gates without clearance to test his limits. The facility flagged it. He ignored the flags. The energy wasn't just augmenting him anymore — it was replacing him, tissue by tissue, thought by thought, the gate rewriting everything it touched.

The last mission on record went unanswered. No extraction. No debrief. The facility sealed his file and quietly redirected funding.

What came back from that gate was not Cael Vrenne.

Vrael does not remember the lab. Does not remember the missions or the briefings or the moment someone finally said his name with something like respect. The gate finished what he started — took everything he fed it and left nothing recognisable in return.

He is what the City Breach built itself around. The first thing hunters meet when they step through a portal. Not a monster the gate created. A man who handed himself to it, piece by piece, in exchange for being seen.

The irony is complete. He is famous now. Just not in any form he can understand.

## Appearance

Vrael is enormous — not born that way, built that way by the absorption. His frame expanded as the gate energy restructured his body over months of unsanctioned exposure. What remains of his original form is buried under layers of fused gate matter and cracked tactical armour from his final mission, partially melted into his skin.

- **Body:** Hulking, wide, forward-leaning. The posture of something that charges rather than thinks.
- **Armour:** Burnt tactical plate, mission-era issue, no longer removable — fused directly to the chest and shoulders. A partial facility ID tag is still visible on the left shoulder plate, serial number illegible, edges burned away.
- **Skin:** Where the armour ends, orange-red cracked skin begins — gate energy fractures running through like fault lines, glowing amber-white from underneath. Not decorative. Structural.
- **Face:** No helmet. The upper face is mostly gone — consumed by ember and gate matter. Lower jaw remains, fused shut. No expression is possible. A permanent low glow pulses from the eye sockets.
- **Eyes:** Burning orange, no pupils, constant low ember glow. Not watching. Just burning.
- **Aura:** Deep orange with white-hot crack lines radiating outward — mirrors Vesol's cold-to-crimson shift but locked permanently at the violent end. Uncontrolled. No discipline. Just output.

## Combat

### Phase 1 — 100–60% HP
Vrael is aggressive and direct. No feints. No reads. Pure forward pressure.

| Attack | Description | CD | Telegraph | Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Flame Charge | Full lane dash in a straight line, leaves fire trail | 7s | Body leans back, amber glow builds for 1s | Side dodge |
| Swipe | Wide melee arc, 180°, single hit | 4s | Arm extends wide, 600ms pause | Back off or dodge through |

### Phase 2 — 60–0% HP
The gate energy destabilises. Vrael loses what little pattern he had.

| Attack | Description | CD | Telegraph | Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fire Pools | Drops persistent DoT zones on floor, lasts 8s | 10s | Brief full-body flare before drop | Benzu tank / jump over |
| Flame Charge+ | Same as Phase 1 but faster, leaves wider fire trail | 6s | Same telegraph, shorter window | Tight dodge timing |

**Phase transition:** Brief slow-mo, Vrael stops mid-arena, aura flares white — the only moment of stillness in the entire fight. Then Phase 2 begins.

**Counter hint:** Vesol burn synergy — burn stacks amplify all damage. Prioritise landing burn early.

### Co-op Scaling
- 2P: +50% HP
- 3P: +100% HP
- 4P: +150% HP + extra fire pool spawns per charge

---

## Design Notes

- Vrael should feel like a runaway engine — no pause, no hesitation, constant forward momentum
- The stillness at phase transition is intentional and should feel wrong. One beat of quiet in an otherwise relentless fight.
- His ID tag on the shoulder is a visual detail for players who look. Not highlighted. No tooltip. Just there.
- Death animation: gate energy destabilises outward, cracks spread across full body, collapses inward. No explosion — implosion. The gate takes back what it gave.
