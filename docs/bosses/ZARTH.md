# ZARTH — Earth Tank

**Zone:** Ruin Den  
**Type:** Earth Tank  
**Hunter Echo:** Benzu — strength without warmth. Benzu builds and protects. Zarth is what remains when a place survives long enough to forget people entirely.

---

## Quote

> *"Don't listen. The walls remember."*
> — Zarth, recorded during a containment breach.

---

## Lore

The Ruin Den was not always a boss arena. It was a disaster site.

Deep below the city, a gate opened during early excavation work and the collapse that followed buried the whole level in concrete, steel, and stone. Some died in the first breach. Some in the sealing attempts. Some in the years after, trapped by the shifting weight of the ruin while the gate energy kept leaking into the fractures below.

What grew there was not a creature in the usual sense. It was the accumulation of impact, grief, pressure, and gate radiation given shape. The more the structure collapsed, the more alive it became. The more people died there, the more it learned the shape of holding on.

Zarth is the place itself waking up.

He has no origin point you can point to, no human face beneath it, no moment of birth. He was formed by the ruin, then hardened by every life lost inside it. The walls became muscle. The foundation became bone. The silence became intent.

That is why he mirrors Benzu so completely. Benzu is a builder, a protector, a man who meets force with force because he still believes in people. Zarth is the same scale of presence with all of the humanity stripped away. He does not rage. He does not hunt. He endures, and he denies passage.

The Ruin Den is not his lair.

It is him.

Every rubble column is a rib. Every collapsed tunnel is a memory. Every crack in the floor is a record of something that ended here and never left. The grunts he summons in Phase 2 are not reinforcements — they are fragments of the structure breaking off and moving. Parts of Zarth, sent forward.

He does not want to destroy the hunters.

He wants them to stop going deeper.

---

## Appearance

Zarth is architectural. He reads less like a monster and more like a section of the world that stood up and decided to stay that way.

- **Body:** Massive, blocky, immovable. The silhouette is all load-bearing shapes — shoulders like support beams, torso like a collapsed pillar held together by force of will rather than engineering.
- **Surface:** Cracked stone, packed earth, concrete plates, and rusted reinforcement bars fused into one body. Dust never quite falls off him — it hangs in the air around him constantly.
- **Face:** Not truly a face — a carved, ruin-like front with no expression possible. Features are deep grooves in stone rather than flesh. There is no mouth. There is nothing to say.
- **Eyes:** Two narrow red fissures buried inside the front mass. They glow only when he is about to strike or shift terrain. Otherwise dark, barely visible, easy to miss.
- **Aura:** Deep red-gold fractures running through the body — same palette as Benzu's aura but dead and static. No pulse. No shimmer. Just pressure trapped in stone, like heat that has nowhere to go.
- **Movement:** Minimal. Each step sends vibration through the floor before the foot lands. When he moves it matters.

## Combat

Zarth establishes the arena as hostile. His attacks are about position and pressure rather than speed. He does not chase. He restructures the space until there is nowhere left to stand.

### Phase 1 — 100–60% HP
Slow, deliberate, building pressure.

| Attack | Description | CD | Telegraph | Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Slam | Overhead impact sends shockwave across the full lane | 8s | Both arms rise slowly, red fissures brighten | Range poke / step back |
| Arm Wall | Raises a stone barrier that blocks movement and pushes players back | 6s | Shoulders lock, rubble lifts from ground in a line | Wait for gap / stun break |

### Phase 2 — 60–0% HP
The ruin becomes unstable. Zarth starts using the environment as an extension of himself.

| Attack | Description | CD | Telegraph | Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rubble Spawn | Ejects stone fragments that stagger players; summons 2 grunts in 4P | 12s | Floor cracks spread from beneath his body | Burst down spawns / reposition |
| Crumble Pulse | Wider shockwave that shakes the lane and dislodges edge rubble | 10s | Full-body dim red flare, then one beat of silence | Jump timing / spacing |
| Slam+ | Faster overhead impact, wider shockwave radius | 7s | Same telegraph, shorter window | Tight dodge timing |

**Phase transition:** The arena quiets for one full beat. The lights in his fissures go almost completely dark before surging back red-gold. The dust in the air around him thickens.

**Counter hint:** Sereisa slow. Benzu shoulder charge can stagger wall formations and interrupt Arm Wall mid-raise.

### Co-op Scaling
- 2P: +50% HP
- 3P: +100% HP
- 4P: +150% HP + additional rubble spawns per Crumble Pulse

## Death — The Ruin Settles

When the final hit lands, Zarth does not fall dramatically.

He lowers. Slowly, like a building finally completing a collapse it has been resisting for years. The red-gold fissures go dark one by one — starting at the extremities, working inward toward the chest. Each one out is a silence added to a silence.

The last fissure — the deepest one, centre of the torso — holds for a moment longer than the others.

Then it goes dark.

The dust in the air settles. For the first time in the entire fight the air in the Ruin Den is still.

The rubble that made up his body does not disappear. It remains on the floor of the arena. The hunters are standing in the ruins of something that was already ruins.

The exit portal opens.

---

## Design Notes

- Zarth should feel like a problem that cannot be argued with. Patience is the only answer.
- His movement budget is deliberately minimal. The moments when he does move should feel like events.
- The dust ambient around him should be constant throughout the fight — a low particle presence that never stops. It is part of him.
- The grunts spawned in Phase 2 should visually match his material — stone fragments with red fissures, not standard grunt skins. They are him, not reinforcements.
- Death sequence has no music sting. Just the fissures going out one by one and then the sound of the dust settling.
- The rubble remains on the arena floor after death. No cleanup. The hunters walk out through what used to be Zarth.
