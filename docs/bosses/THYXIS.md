# THYXIS — Thunder Beast

**Zone:** Thunder Spire  
**Type:** Thunder Beast  
**Hunter Echo:** Sereisa — lightning without control. Sereisa is a human who mastered something ancient and electric through discipline and will. Thyxis is an ancient force that found a pure vessel and inhabits it without ego, without intent. Both are lightning. One chose it. One became it without asking.

---

> *"In the old texts there is no name for it. Only a shape in the storm. Only the silence after."*

## Lore

Before the gates, Thyxis did not exist in this world. Not as a body, not as a beast. Only as an essence — ancient, formless, the residue of something that had been worshipped and forgotten across so many civilisations that no single tradition remembered it whole. A storm god in one language. A sky guardian in another. An omen in a third. The same shape kept appearing in old records that had no reason to share a source.

When the gates opened and raw primordial energy began bleeding into the world, that essence stirred. It had no agenda. No anger. No memory of being forgotten. It simply recognised that the world had enough energy to hold it again and moved toward the place where that energy was most concentrated — the Thunder Spire, a gate-adjacent structure already saturated with atmospheric charge and wild lightning.

At the peak of the Spire lived a wolf.

Not a gate creature. Not an aberration. A guardian — old, solitary, bound to that place by something older than the gates. It had been there so long that the hunters who first surveyed the Spire noted it in field reports as a local anomaly and moved on. It did not attack. It watched. It stayed.

The essence of Thyxis found it and the wolf did not run.

Whether the wolf understood what it was accepting is not something anyone can know. What happened is this: the gate energy surged, the essence descended, and the wolf became the vessel. Not corrupted. Not destroyed. Chosen. The wolf's nature — its guardianship, its loyalty to place, its refusal to leave — became the foundation that Thyxis inhabited. The ancient power filled the animal completely.

Thyxis does not hunt. Does not pursue. Does not leave the Thunder Spire.

The wolf wouldn't.

There is no malice in the fight. No intent to destroy. When hunters enter the Spire they are entering the wolf's territory and Thyxis is what answers. The storm is not angry. It is just what the wolf has become — overwhelming, ancient, and completely without hesitation.

**When Thyxis is defeated, the essence dissipates.** Pulled back out of the physical world without a vessel to hold it, it dissolves into the storm. The gate energy recedes.

What remains is the wolf.

It drops to the floor of the Spire and the lightning fades and the fur goes quiet and it lies there breathing. Exhausted. Confused. Just an animal again, in the place it has always protected, doing the only thing it has ever known how to do.

Staying.

## Appearance

Thyxis reads as a natural force given shape — not manufactured, not corrupted. Something that has always existed and was never meant to be faced.

- **Body:** Large fox-wolf hybrid, four-legged but enormous — twice the height of a hunter at the shoulder. The frame is lean and fast, built for aerial movement and sudden direction changes.
- **Fur:** Pale blue-white, crackling with constant low-level charge. Static electricity lifts individual strands permanently. In Phase 3 the fur shifts from pale blue to blinding full white — the wolf barely visible inside the light.
- **Horns:** Two curved horns rising from the skull, bone-white, arcing thin lightning between them constantly. The arc intensifies with each phase.
- **Eyes:** Electric white-blue, pupils slit like a wolf's. The only part of the design that reads as purely animal — watchful, present, not hostile.
- **Aura:** Blue-white that mirrors Sereisa's yellow-white crackling, shifted colder. In Phase 3 it becomes blinding full white, radiating outward with no defined edge. Natural. Overwhelming.
- **Movement:** Fast and aerial — the heaviest hits come from above, not from the sides.

## Combat

### Phase 1 — 100–70% HP
Thyxis is testing the space. Measured, powerful, not yet fully committed.

| Attack | Description | CD | Telegraph | Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ground Pound | Leaps and lands with a shockwave that crosses the full lane | 8s | Rises fully off ground, blue aura pulls inward before impact | Dodge / Stun ground |
| Claw Combo | Three-hit sweeping claw sequence, wide arc | 5s | Head lowers, ears flatten, 600ms pause | Back off between hits |

### Phase 2 — 70–40% HP
The storm responds to the pressure. Thyxis becomes aerial and erratic.

| Attack | Description | CD | Telegraph | Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bolt Dive | Homing aerial dive wrapped in lightning — tracks nearest player | 6s | Rises to top of lane, fur flares bright, locks target | Slow / Last-second dodge |
| Chain Lightning | Arc that jumps between 2–4 players simultaneously | 10s | Horns charge fully, arc between them maximises | Spread out / Platforms |

### Phase 3 — 40–0% HP
The wolf and the essence are at their limit. The Spire itself becomes hostile.

| Attack | Description | CD | Telegraph | Counter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Storm DoT Zones | Persistent floor lightning patches, 8s duration | 12s | Floor brightens in patches before strike | Constant repositioning |
| Grunts | ×2 gate creatures summoned in 4P | 15s | Secondary gate energy pulse from Spire walls | Burst down fast |
| Bolt Dive+ | Faster, tighter tracking than Phase 2 | 5s | Same telegraph, shorter window | Burn amp + Ultimates |

**Phase transitions:**
- **70% HP:** The wolf howls once — the only sound it makes in the entire fight. The storm doubles in intensity.
- **40% HP:** The fur goes white. The arena dims around the blinding aura. The wolf is almost invisible inside the light.

**Counter hint:** Sereisa slow. Burn amplification from Vesol. All ultimates on Phase 3.

### Co-op Scaling
- 2P: +50% HP
- 3P: +100% HP
- 4P: +150% HP + chain lightning always hits maximum players + extra storm zones

## Death — The Wolf Returns

Thyxis has no death animation in the traditional sense.

When the final hit lands, the essence of Thyxis destabilises. The lightning pulls inward. The white aura collapses. The horns go dark. The fur settles from blinding white back through pale blue and then to the grey-brown of a natural wolf coat.

Thyxis drops to the floor of the Spire.

Not an explosion. Not an implosion. Not a dissolve. Just a large wolf, lying on the stone, breathing.

The exit portal opens. The wolf does not move to stop anyone from leaving.

It stays.

---

## Design Notes

- The howl at 70% HP is the only audio cue in the fight that doesn't come from an attack. It should feel completely different — quieter, older, not threatening.
- The wolf's eyes should remain constant across all three phases. Everything else changes. The eyes don't. They are the through-line.
- The death sequence should have no music sting. Just the ambient storm fading and then the sound of the wolf breathing.
- Players who did not read the lore will see a boss defeated. Players who did will understand what they are looking at.
